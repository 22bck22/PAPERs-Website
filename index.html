The PAPERS Curriculum

A year-long research framework spanning three academic terms — from formulating your first question to submitting your final manuscript.

Year-Long Curriculum

From question to publication.

Term 01 Fall
Fall Term
Research Outline
September — December

Students develop focused research questions and organize their ideas into a structured plan. By the end of fall term, every student has a clear research roadmap that guides their project from start to finish — including a defined scope, research question, and initial bibliography.

🔍
Question Formulation
Narrowing broad curiosity into a specific, answerable research question.
📚
Literature Review
Surveying existing scholarship to identify gaps and position your research.
🗺️
Research Roadmap
Building a structured plan with milestones, timelines, and scope.
Fall Deliverables
Annotated bibliography (12–15 sources)
Research question statement and justification
Project outline and scope document
Peer feedback on research question
Term 02 Winter
Winter Term
Methodology
January — March

Students explore a variety of research methods as they design their studies, applying appropriate tools to collect and analyze data. Winter term is the most technically rigorous phase — students learn to match their questions to the right methodological approach, whether quantitative, qualitative, or mixed-methods.

⚗️
Research Design
Choosing between experimental, observational, survey, or archival approaches.
📊
Data Collection
Designing instruments — surveys, protocols, datasets — and collecting evidence.
📈
Data Analysis
Applying appropriate analytical frameworks — statistical, thematic, or computational.
Winter Deliverables
Methodology section draft
Data collection instrument or protocol
Preliminary results / data set
Peer methodological review
Term 03 Spring
Spring Term
Drafting & Editing
April — June

Students turn their work into clear, well-structured drafts and refine them through iterative peer review. Spring term culminates in each student presenting findings to both academic and public audiences — and, for those ready, submitting to external journals with PAPERS support.

✍️
Academic Writing
Crafting clear, concise, and properly cited academic manuscripts.
🔄
Peer Review
Structured peer critique sessions modeled on journal review processes.
🎤
Public Presentation
Communicating findings to academic and general audiences with confidence.
Spring Deliverables
Full manuscript draft (complete paper)
Two rounds of structured peer review
Public or academic presentation
Final manuscript + journal submission (if ready)
Our Approach

Built on three principles.

PAPERS isn't a class — it's a research community. Everything we do is grounded in these commitments.

I
Student-Led Discovery
PAPERS guides but doesn't prescribe. Every research question comes from the student. Advisors ask questions; students find the answers.
II
Rigorous Peer Review
Peer review sessions are structured to mirror real academic processes — constructive, rigorous, and designed to strengthen every paper.
III
Publication as a Goal
We treat every project as though it will be published. Whether or not it reaches a journal, students build work they're proud to share with the world.

The PAPERS Chapter Model

PAPERS is designed to be replicated. The three-term curriculum is structured, documented, and portable — any school with motivated students and faculty support can launch a chapter. Our framework travels with you.

Chapters access shared resources including curriculum guides, peer review rubrics, presentation templates, and access to the PAPERS journal for student publication.
